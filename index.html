Interactive Experience Research

Assignment 1 | Interactive Media

Student Name: Jiashuo Liu

Target Site: Palat 2023

1. First Impression & Attention

Upon landing on the website, my attention was immediately seized by the aggressive and high-contrast color palette, featuring vibrant pinks, yellows, and blues. The large, bold typography that shifts its messaging dynamically further dominated my visual field, creating an immediate sense of energy and a non-traditional aesthetic that distinguishes this site from standard professional portfolios.

2. Two-Minute Interaction Log

During my two-minute interaction, I began by scrolling vertically to observe the shifting background colors and typography. I then paused to hover my cursor over the floating white triangle in the "Perfectly center div" section to see its physics-based reaction. Following this, I spent significant time moving my mouse across the interactive particle grid in the middle of the page to watch the pixels displace. Finally, I scrolled to the bottom to interact with the scattered contact information letters and clicked the "LINE" link to trigger the appearance of a QR code overlay.

3. Most Engaging Component

The section I spent the most time engaging with was the interactive "Pixels Displacement" grid. This part of the experience felt like a digital sandbox where the movement of the cursor physically pushed the white squares away, creating a tactile and responsive visual effect that encouraged prolonged experimentation with different mouse speeds and patterns.

4. Common Action Pattern

The most common action throughout my interaction was rapid and continuous mouse movement. Because almost every element of the site—from the floating shapes to the scattered text and the particle grid—responds directly to the cursor's coordinates, I found myself constantly moving the mouse to trigger the hidden interactive layers of the design.

5. Primary Goal Perception

My impression is that the primary goal of this experience is to serve as a "living resume" that demonstrates the creator's advanced technical skills in frontend development and creative coding. Rather than just listing skills, the website exists to prove that Palat can construct unimaginable and artistic digital environments that push the boundaries of traditional web design.

6. Goal Communication

The experience communicates this goal through its sheer technical complexity and the subversion of standard web conventions. By making every part of the interface interactive and visually loud, it forces the user to acknowledge the developer's mastery over CSS, physics engines, and JavaScript, effectively transforming the site itself into the most important piece of work in the portfolio.

7. Temporal Interaction Impression

I believe this experience is designed for a high-intensity but short-term interaction. It is likely intended to be explored thoroughly once or twice by a potential employer or collaborator for about five to ten minutes, rather than being a destination that users would return to daily for new content.

8. Communicating Temporal Use

The site communicates its one-off nature through its structure as a single-page portfolio. Once the user has explored all the interactive tricks and reached the contact section at the bottom, the narrative arc of the experience is complete, suggesting that the site has fulfilled its purpose of leaving a lasting first impression.

9. Media References

The experience draws heavy references from 20th-century Pop Art, 1990s "Web 1.0" aesthetics, and interactive museum installations. The use of bold, flat color blocks and oversized text mimics poster design, while the interactive particle effects feel reminiscent of modern digital art exhibits where human motion influences projected visuals.

10. Behavioral Cues from References

These references communicate that I should act as an explorer rather than a passive reader. The toy-like quality of the interactive elements, inspired by digital installations, signals that it is okay to play with the interface, break the alignment of the text, and treat the website as an interactive playground.

11. Emotional Cues from References

The combination of retro aesthetics and modern code evokes a feeling of nostalgic excitement and creative freedom. It makes the engagement feel less like a formal business transaction and more like an invitation to witness a personal artistic statement, fostering a sense of curiosity and playfulness.

12. Frustrating Elements

The most frustrating part of the interaction to me was the readability of the contact section where the letters scatter and move away from the mouse. While visually impressive, this interaction made it difficult to quickly find and click the specific contact links, as the text was constantly evading my cursor, which felt counterintuitive when I actually wanted to perform a functional task.

13. Satisfying Elements

The most satisfying part was the "Pixels Displacement" section due to its high level of responsiveness and fluid feel. There was a direct and instantaneous correlation between my physical hand movement and the digital reaction of the grid, providing a sense of power and haptic feedback that is often missing from traditional, static web pages.
