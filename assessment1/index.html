Interactive Experience Research

Interactive Media

Jiashuo Liu

Website: palat2023.com

1. Initial Attention

The first elements that grab attention are the oversized typography and the high-contrast color scheme. The combination of bright pink and yellow backgrounds creates a strong visual impact that immediately signals an unconventional design approach. The dynamic nature of the changing text ensures the viewer remains focused on the screen from the moment the page loads.

2. Two-Minute Interaction

Interaction begins with vertical scrolling to observe the transitions between different color blocks. The experience then leads to hovering over floating shapes to test their physics-based reactions. Significant time is spent exploring the interactive pixel grid where the cursor displaces small squares. The session ends with an attempt to navigate the contact links located at the bottom of the page.

3. Most Engaging Part

The interactive pixel displacement grid is the most engaging component of the entire site. The responsive nature of the grid allows for a sense of play and experimentation. Watching the white squares react to the speed and direction of the mouse provides a tactile digital experience that keeps the visitor interested for a longer period.

4. Most Common Action

Rapid mouse movement is the most frequent action performed during the interaction. Since nearly every visual element on the page responds to the cursor's coordinates, constant movement is required to trigger animations and explore the hidden layers of the interface.

5. Primary Goal

The primary goal of the website appears to be a demonstration of advanced creative coding and technical skills. It serves as a digital proof of concept, showing that the developer can create unimaginable and artistic environments that go beyond the limits of traditional web design.

6. Goal Communication

The goal is communicated through the complexity of the interactive elements. By making the user play with the interface, the developer proves their mastery of JavaScript and CSS physics. The site effectively functions as a live portfolio piece that speaks for itself through its unconventional behavior.

7. Interaction Over Time

This experience is likely intended for high-intensity, short-term engagement. It is a destination meant for a single, thorough exploration lasting around five to ten minutes. It functions as a memorable first impression rather than a site for daily repeat visits.

8. Communicating Temporal Use

The single-page structure communicates this intended use clearly. There is a logical progression from the introductory bold text to the final contact section. Once all interactive "tricks" have been explored and the bottom of the page is reached, the user naturally feels that the experience is complete.

9. Other Media References

The website references a mix of 90s web aesthetics, pop art, and modern digital art installations. The flat color blocks and large fonts remind the observer of poster design, while the particle effects feel similar to interactive exhibits seen in modern galleries.

10. Influence on Action

These references suggest that the viewer should act as an explorer or a player rather than a reader. The toy-like feedback from the interface signals that it is acceptable to move fast, click around, and disrupt the layout to see how the site reacts.

11. Influence on Feeling

The vibrant colors and experimental layout create a feeling of curiosity and creative freedom. The interaction feels less like a formal task and more like an invitation to explore a personal artistic statement, making the engagement feel fresh and exciting.

12. Most Frustrating Part

The most frustrating element is the contact section where the links move away from the mouse. While the effect is technically impressive, it makes the functional task of clicking a link difficult. This evasive behavior can be annoying when the viewer is trying to perform a specific action like sending an email.

13. Most Satisfying Part

The high level of responsiveness throughout the site is the most satisfying part of the experience. The instant correlation between mouse movement and on-screen reaction provides a fluid and polished feel. This zero-lag feedback gives the website a sense of high quality and technical precision.
